## Overview

The Superstore Sales Performance Dashboard is an interactive Microsoft Excel project focused on analyzing sales, profit, regional performance, category contribution, customer segments, discount impact, and overall business performance using the Superstore dataset.

Using **Microsoft Excel 365**, I cleaned and structured the raw sales data, created calculated fields, built pivot tables and pivot charts, designed two dashboard pages, and summarized the results into a final insights page with business recommendations.

The project demonstrates how Excel can be used to transform transactional sales data into a clean, interactive, and decision-ready business intelligence dashboard.

## Tools

Microsoft Excel 365 | Excel Tables | Pivot Tables | Pivot Charts | Slicers | Data Cleaning | Dashboard Design | Business Analysis

## What I Did

- Cleaned and structured the raw Superstore sales dataset in Excel
- Converted the cleaned dataset into an Excel Table for easier analysis and dynamic referencing
- Created additional analytical fields such as year, month, year-month, shipping days, profit margin, profit status, and discount level
- Built pivot tables to summarize sales, profit, quantity, category performance, regional performance, customer segments, and discount impact
- Designed an executive dashboard page showing headline KPIs and high-level business performance
- Designed a detailed dashboard page showing segment performance, discount impact, sub-category sales, and profitability breakdown
- Added slicers to support interactive filtering by key dimensions
- Developed an insights page summarizing findings and recommendations for business decision-making

## Key Findings

- Total sales were approximately **$2.30M**, with total profit of about **$286K**
- The overall profit margin was approximately **12.5%**
- The **West** region recorded the strongest sales performance
- **Technology** was the strongest category in terms of profitability
- **Furniture** generated strong sales but weaker profit, suggesting margin pressure
- **No Discount** orders produced the highest profit
- **Medium** and **High Discount** orders reduced profitability
- The **Consumer** segment contributed strongly to overall sales and profit

## Recommendations

- Reduce unnecessary medium and high discounting to protect profitability
- Review Furniture pricing, cost, and discount strategy to improve margins
- Prioritize Technology products in sales and marketing campaigns
- Strengthen sales efforts in weaker-performing regions, especially the South
- Monitor discount levels before approving large or strategic orders
- Use dashboard slicers to regularly review performance by year, region, category, and segment
